Recently™ I have started telling people "I love music". I've probably always thought that way, for as long as I can remember at least, but I have never realized it was a part of me. "Doesn't every one love music?" A thought I remember frequently having. I wouldn't say I'm "musical", yet most of my deep connections have blossomed through a shared love of music and this dissonance has made it hard for me to realize this. In living my life, I noticed I take pride in being uniquely creative. The sum of my past experiences have allowed me to view situations in a certain manner, and I should listen to that perspective when creating things. Yet I don't feel like I have a unique creativity for music, I can only create things I've heard in the past. Really I hope that not all forms of thought must result in some unique creation, as the music I listen to was created for others to join in their experience. I've realized, along with many other hobbies, that to love music you mustn't have the skills or creativity to create something just the ears and heart to understand.

Of course... music is meant to be listened to and I love listening to music.
